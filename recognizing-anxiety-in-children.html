Recognizing Anxiety in Children

Anxiety is common among children and often goes unnoticed. As a parent or educator, recognizing early signs is key to offering timely support.

Common Signs of Anxiety in Kids

  • Frequent complaints of headaches or stomachaches with no medical cause
  • Difficulty sleeping or nightmares
  • Excessive worrying about school performance or social situations
  • Avoiding school or social events
  • Overreacting to small problems
  • Seeking constant reassurance

What You Can Do

  • Validate their feelings - do not dismiss their fears
  • Create a calm and safe environment
  • Encourage open conversations
  • Use books or stories to talk about anxiety
  • Consider professional support if symptoms persist

Quick Tip
